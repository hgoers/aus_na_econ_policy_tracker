## PRIORITY INTELLIGENCE

### LNG Supply Shock Creates Australian Export Opportunity

**Development**: QatarEnergy declared force majeure on liquefied natural gas shipments Wednesday evening following Iranian drone attacks on production facilities. The state-owned company, which supplies 82% of its LNG to Asian markets, expects production restoration to take "at least one month."

**Scale and Duration**: 
- Qatar accounts for approximately 22% of global LNG supply (~77 MMtpa capacity affected)
- Disruption timeline extends through Q2 2026 at minimum
- Asian markets face immediate supply shortfall in Japan, South Korea, China

**Australian Trade Impact**:
- **Immediate pricing uplift**: Spot LNG prices expected to strengthen significantly, benefiting JCC-linked Australian contracts
- **Volume opportunity**: Australian LNG projects positioned to capture displaced Qatar supply to Asian customers
- **Revenue estimate**: Potential $200-400 million monthly uplift for Australian LNG sector during disruption period
- **Strategic positioning**: Reinforces Australia's role as reliable alternative supplier to major Asian importers

**Commercial implications for Australian exporters**: Gorgon, Wheatstone, Ichthys, and APLNG projects likely to see immediate commercial benefit through both spot price improvements and potential contract renegotiation opportunities.
