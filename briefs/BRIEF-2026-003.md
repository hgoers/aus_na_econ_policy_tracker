## LEAD

Australia faces $11+ billion in new US tariff exposure as the Trump administration implements a 15% global tariff "this week," despite two decades of preferential treatment under AUSFTA. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent confirmed Wednesday that the levy—affecting all trading partners—will likely take effect by March 10, marking the first broad-based tariff imposition on Australian goods since the free trade agreement's 2005 implementation. With Australian goods exports to the US totalling $74.4 billion in the latest data, the 15% duty would impose approximately $11.2 billion in annual tariff costs, fundamentally altering export competitiveness across all sectors from resources to agriculture.

## KEY DEVELOPMENTS — UNITED STATES  

- **Global tariffs** Trump's 15% tariff on all imports advances despite AUSFTA protections, with Treasury Secretary Bessent confirming implementation "this week." Australia joins Canada and other treaty allies in losing preferential access after the Supreme Court struck down Trump's original higher tariff program in February. The $11+ billion exposure represents approximately 15% of total Australian goods exports to the US, creating material margin compression for exporters across all sectors.

- **Legal challenges** Twenty US states filed suit yesterday to block Trump's tariff revival, though immediate injunctive relief appears unlikely given the administration's use of temporary emergency authorities. The effective date remains unclear pending formal Federal Register publication, but Bessent's timeline suggests March 10 implementation. Australian exporters face regulatory uncertainty as legal challenges develop.
